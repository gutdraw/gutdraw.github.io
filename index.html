Taxes

Taxation is a power held by the legislative branch, not the executive branch however presidential candidates are often asked to purpose a tax plan. Here is mine:

Federal Revenues as a percentage of GDP cannot be expected to deviate far from the historical par of 17.5%. source
Net Federal Outlays as a percentage of GDP cannot be expected to deviate far from the 2015 levels of 20.5%. source
This leaves an annual nominal deficit of 3% of GDP.
Current inflation trends would indicate a rate of 1% (roughly the current level) up to the Federal Reserve Bank’s target of 2% in the medium term.
This means Real GDP Growth (which excludes inflation) must be 2% to 1 % to prevent growth of national debit as a percentage of GDP.
Current real GDP growth is somewhere around 1.4%. Accelerating growth rate will be required to reduce national debit as a percentage of GDP.
Current corporate tax revenue is expected to be an estimated 2.5% of GDP in 2016, whereas personal income taxes are estimated at 8.7% of GDP for 2016. source
This means a roughly 5.8% increase in personal income tax revenue could offset a 20% reduction in corporate tax revenue.

  • Corporate tax rate should be reduced by a fifth (to 28% from 34% to 35%) offset by increases in personal tax rates of high earners to establish a revenue neutral change.
Because the top 2.7% of tax payers pay 51.6% of the personal income tax, personal taxes revenue should be increased in the following ways:
  • 4% surcharge income tax on wages above $1 million
  • raising the investment income rate from 20% to 25%
  • carried interest income is treated as wages above $1 million
Net corporate profits would increase; if those are distributed as dividends tax is recouped, if they are kept on the balance sheet the barrier to capital investment has been lowered by the tax rate reduction.
The intent of all of this is to increase monetary velocity and therefore growth.

Shift tax credits from special interests to working families
  • Reduction/Removal of special interest tax breaks such as Alcohol Fuel Credit, Accelerated Depreciation, and Title Passage Rule excess foreign tax credits
  • Expand/Provide child care and educational expense tax credits

Climate Change

Climate Change is real, influenced by human activity, dangerous, and requires unparalleled international cooperation to address. Both the Republican Bush Administration [2008] and Democratic Obama Administration [2014] have pledged funding for the UN’s GCF [Green Climate Fund] and I would insist on this continued financial support.

Unfortunately we are already past the point where slowing of emissions alone can reach our previously internationally committed goals from the Paris Agreement. NETs [Negative Emission Technologies] will be required to prevent the most catastrophic scenarios. The U.S. as a world leader in innovation, research, and technology must take a leadership role in developing NETs. The funding for this should come from the revenue generated from drilling and mining on public lands which amounted to $9.6 Billion Dollar in FY2015. source Additionally the rate charged for lease on these public lands needs increased up to market rate oppose to the artificially low rate currently (a back door subsidy); royalty rates are also artificially low and need to be negotiated competitively in accordance with market rates. These advantageous rates no longer can be justified with the removal of crude oil export ban in the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2016 and as our net energy imports approaches zero. source

With the additional revenue from public lands use I would love to see coal industry labor staff retrained in BECCS [Bioenergy with carbon capture and storage] as market forces push the nation away from coal (largely because of cheaper natural gas) as an example of creating jobs and helping the environment simultaneously.

Healthcare

Healthcare in America is broken and has been for quite some time. Additionally it must be recognized there is a fundamental problem with insurance, for it to be successful most of the people have to pay in more than they claim out; therefore even in a completely unregulated market it would still be a mechanism of redistribution of wealth from most healthy to least healthy. The ACA [Affordable Care Act] has pro and cons; anyone denying the existence of either is blinded by partisan bias. That said; I see the need for significant changes to healthcare in America.
Two Provisions I take issue with in the ACA are likely some of the least known; Transitional Reinsurance and Temporary Risk Corridors. They can be detailed here
In essence for the first three years of the ACA implementation the most profitable insurance firms would subsidize the most unprofitable insurance firms. This is very flawed for many reason but some are:
  • This causes a cliff, when this 3 year period ends, where the risk makeup of the insurance market changes significantly (2018)
  • This required government involvement in the actuarial calculations of insurance plans and the results compared to those predictions. This is not an exact science and highly complex.
  • This creates a moral hazard where firms try to make just enough profit as to not have to pay into the subsidy fund.
  • These structures were to be neutral to the Federal Budget, but because of this mandate and actual profit loss results, the programs have not been able to deliver on its stated promises leading to lawsuits from insurers for lack of loss reimbursement payments.source In my view this broken promise also played a role in many insurers leaving the insurance exchanges for 2017 insurance offerings.
  • These programs were all said to indirectly help Americans have more affordable insurance and better options, but in my view this was an assumption which lacks concrete and direct evidence.
I would propose an alternative risk managing mechanism. America has always rallied around those facing a catastrophe. Whether a tornado, hurricane, terror attack, or wild fires Federal Funds have been used knowing those in need would receive it. This concept needs to be applied in healthcare. Catastrophic health insurance coverage (i.e. individual reinsurance) should be provided by the federal government. If an individual has greater than $100K (index to inflation with a factor for amount paid in benefits from the prior rolling 3 year period for future adjustment to this threshold) in non-elective medical expenses paid/due to providers, the amount above $100k is paid by the Federal government oppose to the insurance firm. Funding for such a benefit would come from reducing the tax credits currently provided for buying insurance from the ACA exchanges and capping the ESI [Employer Sponsored Insurance] tax exclusion as proposed by Speaker Paul Ryan’s Plan. source pg.15 To be clear I am not supporting the Speaker’s whole plan just this one aspect. I also want to make clear this back-stop benefit is not Universal healthcare nor is it a public option. None the less this policy would reduce out of pocket costs for middle class Americans even with the reduction in tax credit due to the following:
  • Reduced exposure to large losses by insurance firms leading to lower barriers to entry to the market and greater competition
  • Without having to pay for private reinsurance costs for insurance firms reduce
  • When combined with the existing 80/20 rule from the ACA (at least 80 of premiums collected must go to pay medical claims or refund checks must be paid to return to this ratio) health insures become low risk/ stable return businesses leading to lower cost of capital for the firm (similar to utilities)
  • Aggregate insurer paid medical claims drops which due to the 80/20 rule mean aggregate premium payments drop
Positive accepts of ACA which must be maintained:
  • Non-Discrimination Provisions (i.e. women can’t be charged more for example)
  • Pre-existing Condition Coverage, in my view this means the individual mandate must also be maintained to prevent only gaining coverage after a health issue arises
  • Those under 26 can be covered on their parent’s plans
  • Ban on lifetime/annual limits
  • 100% coverage of preventative services
Additional negative accepts of ACA which need addressed:
  • Removing the Cadillac Tax and replacing is with ESI exclusion capping as proposed by Speaker Paul Ryan. More information on the Cadillac Tax here
  • Rising the threshold for the employer mandate from 50 FTE [full-time equivalent employees] to 200 FTE
    • While the employer mandate seemed well intentioned in practice is has encouraged subcontracting and larger use of part time workers to stay below the 50 FTE threshold, and this is bad for American workers. While this may still be somewhat true at a 200 FTE level firms of this greater size likely will have a higher value proposition for more stable and permanent staff in my view reducing the risk of moral hazard.
Lastly another key to improving Healthcare in America is reducing the cost of services this should be done in the follow ways among others:
  • Lower corporate tax rates as I propose in my tax plan, which will reduce cost of business for providers as well as insurers
  • Medical liability reform
  • Improvements to FDA regulation to reduce the cost of bring drugs and medical devices to market
