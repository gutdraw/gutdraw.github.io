Taxes

Taxation is a power held by the legislative branch, not the executive branch however presidential candidates are often asked to purpose a tax plan. Here is mine
Federal Revenues as a percentage of GDP cannot be expected to deviate far from the historical par of 17.5%.
Net Federal Outlays as a percentage of GDP cannot be expected to deviate far from the 2015 levels of 20.5%.
This leaves an annual nominal deficit of 3% of GDP.
Current inflation trends would indicate a rate of 1% (roughly the current level) up to the Federal Reserve Bankâ€™s target of 2% in the medium term.
This means Real GDP Growth (which excludes inflation) must be 2% to 1 % to prevent growth of national debit as a percentage of GDP.
Current real GDP growth is somewhere around 1.4%. Accelerating growth rate will be required to reduce national debit as a percentage of GDP.

  • Corporate tax rate should be reduced by a fifth (to 28% from 34% to 35%) offset by increases in personal tax rates to establish a revenue neutral change.

Current corporate tax revenue is expected to be an estimated 2.5% of GDP in 2016, whereas personal income taxes are estimated at 8.7% of GDP for 2016.
This means a roughly 5.8% increase in personal income tax revenue could offset a 20% reduction in corporate tax revenue.
Because the top 2.7% of tax payers pay 51.6% of the personal income tax, personal taxes revenue should be increased in the following ways:
  • 4% surcharge income tax on wages above $1million
  • raising the investment income rate from 20% to 25%
  • carried interest income is treated as wages above $1million

Net corporate profits would increase; if those are distributed as dividends tax is recouped, if they are kept on the balance sheet the barrier to capital investment has been lowered by the tax rate reduction.
The intent of all of this is to increase monetary velocity and therefore growth.
